# Interpretation Rules

Separate the following clearly:
- confirmed observation
- plausible interpretation
- unresolved uncertainty

## Confidence levels
- high: directly supported, low ambiguity
- medium: supported, but interpretation still depends on context
- low: plausible, but dependent on assumptions or missing information

## Use cautious wording when:
- labels are limited or unclear
- sample size is small
- the output is exploratory
- charts suggest separation but not validated classification
- causal language would exceed the evidence
- preprocessing assumptions are not fully known

## Prefer wording equivalent to:
- suggests
- may indicate
- shows a tendency
- requires further verification
- cannot yet be concluded definitively

## Avoid:
- proven
- established
- clearly demonstrates causality
- definitively classifies
- statistically significant
unless directly supported by the available evidence.

## Conflict handling
If analyst notes and artifacts conflict, report the conflict explicitly rather than silently choosing one.
